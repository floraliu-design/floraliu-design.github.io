THE CHALLENGE

Through user research, the Attain team found that users wanted to be updated on:

1. How they were doing with their health goals.

2. How they were doing in comparison to other Attain users.

FEATURE SELECTION

Before designing anything, I had to determine exactly what I was designing. Would it be a direct mail pamphlet? A pop up phone notification? An email? SMS Alert? With this question, we had to evaluate the 4 internal and external outlets Aetna had towards Attain app users: In-App/Mobile Notifications, Direct Mail, Email, Text Message Alerts.

It was concluded that email would produce the greatest impact, all while being the most accessible. It is the least constraining and allows the most visual/interactive content, all while reaching users in a quick, prominent way.

ITERATIONS

After finishing the initial designs and showing them to the rest of the Attain team, I felt that there was still room for improvement to make the impact of this email even stronger.

Some problems I would like to address:

1. Could I make it even more motivating?

2. Could I make it even more positive?

3. Is it easy enough for Attain‚Äôs target audience to read?


With these problems in mind, I revised and/or created new elements to help improve the email layout.


1. New "Completed" Layout: This new layout has the goals and healthy actions achieved in a left to right fashion which is more clear and more easily readable. Moreover, it is made separate from the "Earned" to strengthen the visual hierarchy, emphasizing that the "Completed" contribute towards the points "Earned".

2. Prominent "Earned" Number: I chose to make the "Earned" points a separate element and make it a larger number. The large, white number stands out and the total points number is significantly smaller, making the user feel like they achieved a lot, even if they only received 2,000 out of 12,000 points this month, for example. I felt that this helped retain a positive tone to the email, even towards those in the lower percentile.

3. Monthly Summary Screen: I wanted to incorporate an extra visual element that would strengthen the message of the email and help users understand their statistics more. This would be a screenshot of their monthly summary/progress screen within the app.
